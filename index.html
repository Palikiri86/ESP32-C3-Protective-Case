Introduction to ESP32-C3 Protective CaseThe ESP32-C3 Zero is a compact and powerful microcontroller board widely used in electronics and IoT projects. Due to its small size and exposed components, it can be easily damaged during handling, storage, or classroom use. To address this issue, a protective case can be designed and produced using 3D printing technology. This project focuses on designing and developing a 3D-printed protective case that safely houses the ESP32-C3 Zero while still allowing full access to essential features such as the USB-C port, pins, and buttons. The design must also consider factors such as durability, airflow, ease of use, and compatibility with classroom 3D printing equipment. Through this project, the design thinking process is applied—from research and idea generation to prototyping, testing, and refinement—to create a final product that is both functional and practical. The goal is to develop a case that not only protects the device but also supports usability and efficient performance in real-world applications.
